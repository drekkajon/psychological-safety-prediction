# Predicting Workplace Psychological Safety Using Social and Demographic Indicators

## Brief Background

Psychological safety is a critical driver of team effectiveness, employee engagement, and organizational performance. Employees who feel safe to express ideas, take risks, and speak openly without fear of negative consequences are more likely to contribute to innovation and collaboration.

Despite its importance, psychological safety is often treated as a qualitative concept rather than something that can be measured and predicted using data. Many organizations rely on surveys or anecdotal feedback but lack predictive insights into which individuals or groups may be at risk of low psychological safety.

This project aims to apply data science methods to identify key predictors of psychological safety using large-scale social and demographic datasets. The goal is to generate actionable insights that can support HR leaders and organizational development practitioners in improving workplace culture and employee outcomes.

## Research Question

What social, demographic, and perception-based factors predict an individual’s level of psychological safety in the workplace?

## Hypothesis

Individuals with lower levels of trust (interpersonal and institutional), lower perceived support, and greater exposure to socioeconomic stressors are more likely to report lower levels of psychological safety.

## Prediction

- Individuals with lower trust and higher perceived instability will have a higher probability of low psychological safety indicators  
- A predictive model will classify individuals into high vs. low psychological safety groups with meaningful accuracy
